## What this is about

The most maddening sales call is the one where the prospect agrees with
everything — great fit, fair price, better than the competition — and
still doesn't buy. "Call back in nine months." The explanation is that
the person with buying power has one to three top priorities, and you
aren't one of them. Nobody wakes up randomly deciding today's the day
to buy; something happens — a moment of struggle that forces action.
The book calls these **inciting events**, and they're what separate a
customer who *could* buy from one who *must* buy now: the website got
hacked and the owner is still shaken; the traffic spike that should
have been the best day of the year crashed the site instead; the SEO
consultant said performance is why the rankings won't move.

This skill maps your inciting events, one keystone at a time — because
every real inciting event couples to a keystone: the keystone means
they could care, the event makes it urgent. Where you have customer
interviews, it harvests the trigger stories customers actually told
you, marked as observed. Where you don't, it works backward through
the book's brainstorm lenses — crises that force action, seasonal and
planning cycles, strategic windows, personal life-changes — marked
honestly as hypotheses to validate. And for each event it asks the
question that makes the file operational: how do you *find* prospects
in this condition — what do they search, where do they post, what
public signals give them away? In advertising, the inciting event
often outperforms the keystone, because it speaks to what the customer
is experiencing right now.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-carol-inciting-events Here's our honed KEYSTONES.md — we also have
a FINAL-REPORT.md from twelve customer interviews. What actually makes
these people buy now?
```

The skill walks your keystones one at a time, harvesting real trigger stories from your interview findings and brainstorming the rest through the book's lenses, recording each event with its evidence status and findability notes in `INCITING-EVENTS.md`.

One practical note: say where you're working. Name a directory (or
point at your existing files) when you invoke, and the skill keeps the
method's files together there — if you don't, it asks before creating
anything.

## From the source

Two sources form the foundation of this skill:

- As in Chapter 3 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *Speaking Carol Into Existence* (section "Inciting Events: Carol's Motivation") — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the inciting-event concept, the coupling-to-keystones rule, the work-backward brainstorm lenses, and the findability question this skill runs.
- As in Chapter 10 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *A multiplier of one's own* — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the customer-interview method that produces the *observed* trigger stories this skill harvests first, before hypothesizing anything.

Supporting sources each supply a piece of the mechanism:

- [**The Iterative-Hypothesis customer development method**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/customer-development/) — how hypothesized inciting events get validated: ask customers what prompted them to start looking, right after purchase, while the answer is fresh.
- [**Specificity: A weapon of mass effectiveness**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/specificity/) — why "a hacked website is a personal violation… you pay a consultant $300 per hour" advertises and "customers get frustrated" doesn't: the event must be vivid enough to recognize yourself in.
