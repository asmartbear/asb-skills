## What this is about

An interview you don't write down is an interview you'll misremember —
confirmation bias will quietly keep the parts you liked. The book's method
keeps a spreadsheet: one row per question, one column per interview. This
skill is that discipline without the spreadsheet: after each conversation,
it takes whatever raw material you have — a transcript, hasty notes — and
distills it into a brief, structured debrief file: one concise answer per
interview question (skipped honestly when you never got there), plus an
addenda section for everything interesting that fit no question, which is
often where the biggest learning hides.

The debrief is deliberately brief — evidence, not prose — and it accumulates:
one file per conversation in a directory that becomes your record of what
real customers actually said, ready to be synthesized into hypothesis
updates as the interviews stack up.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-interview-debrief Here's the transcript from my call with a gym owner
this morning — file it against my QUESTIONS.md.
```

The skill drafts the debrief: one brief answer per question asked (verbatim phrases kept, skipped questions honestly marked), plus an addenda of everything interesting that fit no question. It asks you to fill the gaps the transcript can't supply — ambiguous readings, surprises that never made it into your notes.

One practical note: say where you're working. Name a directory (or
point at your existing files) when you invoke, and the skill keeps the
method's files together there — if you don't, it asks before creating
anything.
