## What this is about

You can't define the customer who loves you for who you are until you
know — honestly — who you are. But "write down our strengths and
weaknesses" is an impossible instruction: nobody knows where to start,
everyone rationalizes, and the loudest voice fills the whiteboard. The
book's answer is to start a level lower, with raw observations, gathered
through twelve detailed, evocative question categories: what customers
praise when they choose you despite your foibles, the complaint you have
no defense against, what you're proud of whether customers care or not,
what distinguishes your most profitable customers from the rest, what you
claim to be great at but aren't, what you envy in competitors, what you'd
honor even if it lost you money.

This skill runs that fact-finding as a working session. Bring a team's
write-storm output and it processes the notes one observation at a time —
clarifying, deduplicating, sharpening; or bring nothing but yourself and
it walks you through the twelve categories directly, pressing every vague
answer into a specific, vivid fact ("customers post screenshots of
support wait times on Reddit," not "support could be better"). Two rules
hold throughout, both from the book: observe like an outside consultant —
behaviors and outcomes, never justifications — and don't evaluate,
don't blame, don't act. Judging what's a strength or weakness is the
next step's job; this one just gets the truth on the record.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-carol-observations We're a 6-person bootstrapped SaaS doing email
deliverability monitoring, 4 years in, ~$50k MRR. I want to figure out
who our ideal customer really is — start with the honest look at us.
```

The skill walks you through the twelve question categories one at a time, pressing each answer into specific, vivid observations and recording them in `OBSERVATIONS.md` as you go. You walk away with the raw material the strengths-and-weaknesses step distills next.

Supporting sources each supply a piece of the mechanism:

- [**Specificity: A weapon of mass effectiveness**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/specificity/) — why every observation must be specific and vivid: generic words are interpreted differently by every reader and carry no evidence.
- [**Fail to face the truth, and you fail**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/failure-to-face-the-truth/) — the posture of the whole exercise: an honest look at what is, before deciding what to do about it.
