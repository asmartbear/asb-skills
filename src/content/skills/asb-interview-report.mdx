## What this is about

Interviews end, and the learning scatters — a hypothesis file only the
founder can decode, debriefs nobody rereads, and a teammate asking
"so what did we actually find out?" This skill closes the method: it
distills everything the interviews produced into one report that says
plainly what you now *know* (including what got disproved — negative
knowledge is knowledge), what you merely *think*, and what remains
untested — every claim tagged with its evidence and the customers'
own words.

The report is built for two readers at once. The top is a summary as
brief as possible without losing anything salient, because that's all
most humans will read. Below it sits the full reference: numbered
findings with voice-counts and verbatim quotes, the vocabulary bank,
and per-area briefs that marshal the findings for the work that comes
next — defining your ideal customer (keystone and deal-breaker
candidates, the inciting-event stories you actually heard), positioning
(their exact words, the higher-level outcome they're really buying),
pricing, marketing and sales, and product priorities. The skill doesn't
do that downstream work — it delivers the evidence those decisions
should be made from, with any of its own interpretations clearly
labeled as interpretation.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-interview-report We've finished the interview round — twelve
debriefs in interviews/, hypotheses all synthesized. Write up what we
found so I can share it with my cofounder and our designer.
```

The skill sweeps your goals, hypotheses, questions, and debriefs, drafts the whole report — brief summary on top, evidence-cited findings and per-area briefs below — and then reviews it with you section by section, refusing to state anything stronger than the evidence supports. You walk away with the one document that turns "we did a bunch of interviews" into input your whole company can act on.

One practical note: say where you're working. Name a directory (or
point at your existing files) when you invoke, and the skill keeps the
method's files together there — if you don't, it asks before creating
anything.

## From the source

Two sources form the foundation of this skill:

- As in Chapter 10 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *A multiplier of one's own* (section "Deciding What to Do: Combining Customer Insights with Multipliers") — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the report is the bridge this chapter describes: validated hypotheses are "the facts of the matter" that strategy gets built from, and this skill packages exactly those facts.
- [**The Iterative-Hypothesis customer development method**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/customer-development/) — the method whose artifacts this report distills, including why "no pattern" is still a finding and why AI may summarize your thinking but not replace it.

Supporting chapters define what the report's downstream briefs must feed:

- As in Chapter 3 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *Speaking Carol Into Existence* — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — keystones, deal-breakers, and inciting events: the ideal-customer exercise this report's ICP brief supplies with interview evidence, including the trigger stories the chapter says to collect by interviewing.
- As in Chapter 2 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *Carol Loves You for You* — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — strengths and weaknesses as the raw material of strategy; the report's evidence of what customers praise, tolerate, and abhor feeds that classification.
- As in Chapter 4 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *A Rose by Any Other Name* — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — positioning built from customers' exact words, the Needs Stack, and vivid specifics: the report's vocabulary bank and positioning brief exist to supply them.
- [**For probabilities, use Fermi numbers, not words**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/probability-words/) — why findings state their strength as "7 of 9," never "probably" or "most": individual interpretations of probability words differ more than the words themselves.
- [**Ignoring the Wisdom of Crowds**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/wisdom-of-crowds/) — when tallies find truth (objective facts, where errors cancel) and when votes only veto — why the report counts voices to validate facts but warns against averaging desires into the bland thing nobody hated.
- [**"It's a Balance" isn't always the answer**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/conflicting-choices/) — split evidence is a segment or a choice, never a mid-point: "it's a balance" is the exact opposite of how findings should be reported.
- [**Specificity: A weapon of mass effectiveness**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/specificity/) — the report's two-reader rule in article form: when brevity is the goal examples are bloat, and everywhere else examples are what make claims believable, hard to counter, and researchable.
