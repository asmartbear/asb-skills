## What this is about

The same product, described two ways, can command eight times the
price. "Cut your ad spend in half" caps what you can charge at a slice
of the savings; "double your leads at the same cost per lead" prices
against growth — and the buyer will happily pay several times more,
because growth is what their CEO celebrates. That's the book's first
positioning rule: never sell saving time or money; sell **more value,
in the currency your customer already measures**. Most copy breaks it
on the first line.

The second rule is that saying the right thing vaguely is the same as
saying nothing. "Easy to use, robust features, customer-first" — the
Opposite Test shreds it (who claims their product is *difficult*?),
and generic words guarantee the reader skims without picturing
anything. Vivid copy is specific copy: "$300 an hour" instead of
"expensive," the benchmark instead of "fast," the changelog instead of
"innovative," the quiet part said out loud.

This skill applies the whole toolbox to your actual copy, one message
at a time: it diagnoses what each message breaks — wrong value
framing, wrong level of your customer's needs, generic words, clichés,
claims that fail the Opposite Test, and mixed price signals (it asks
which of the book's three pricing strategies you run — More for More,
More for Less, or Less for Less — and holds every claim to that one
story, because "highest quality" next to "cheapest" confuses the
buyer into walking away) — then rewrites it with you, pressing for
the true numbers and proof (it never invents a specific; missing
facts go on a verify list). The survivors accumulate in a
reusable statement list covering every role: what you do, what you
promise, what you make obsolete, how you counter the vendors trying to
obviate you, and the inciting-event language that makes a reader say
"that's me." It works markedly better if you bring your ideal-customer
definition and needs stack — and it will tell you so — but it works
without them.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-positioning Here's our homepage hero and three ad variants. We
sell automated backup for dental practices — current headline is
"Enterprise-grade backup, made easy." Our CAROL.md and NEEDS-STACK.md
are in this directory too.
```

The skill walks your messages one at a time — diagnosing each against
the value-framing, level-fit, and vividness rules, rewriting it with
your real facts — and you walk away with a `POSITIONING.md` of
statements sharp enough to reuse in every channel.

One practical note: say where you're working. Name a directory (or
point at your existing files) when you invoke, and the skill keeps the
method's files together there — if you don't, it asks before creating
anything.

## From the source

Two sources form the foundation of this skill:

- As in Chapter 4 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *A Rose by Any Other Name* — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the whole playbook this skill runs: the eight-times-more repositioning story, positioning by needs-stack level, and the Make It Vivid discipline (no generic words, no clichés, show-don't-tell, the Opposite Test and its dominate-the-field exception, the specificity checklist).
- [**How repositioning a product allows you to 8x its price**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/more-value-not-save-money/) — the value-framing rule in full: why growth is worth ten times more than savings to the same buyer, and how the pitch (not the product) sets the price.

Supporting articles each supply a piece of the mechanism:

- [**Specificity: A weapon of mass effectiveness**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/specificity/) — the vividness engine: generic words to specifics, truth to accuracy, concrete examples, and why specificity even makes copy funnier.
- [**The "Opposite Test"**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/opposite-test/) — the claim filter: if no competitor would claim the opposite, you're saying nothing; passing claims have consequences you name and embrace.
- [**"Authentic" is dead. And so is "is dead."**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/authentic-is-dead/) — the cliché graveyard this skill enforces, plus the redemption paths: name & embrace, and owning an abused word with overwhelming proof.
- As in Chapter 6 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *Price Is Not Just a Number* (section "Mixed Signals") — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the three self-consistent pricing strategies (More for More, More for Less, Less for Less) this skill holds every claim to: when price and positioning tell different stories, customers are confused, and confused customers don't buy.
- [**Using the Needs Stack for competitive strategy**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/needs-stack/) — the level-fit framework: features at your level, the promise one level up, and how to position against the vendors obviating you.
- [**Selling to Carol: Why targeting an ICP brings 10x more customers than you expected**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/icp-ideal-customer-persona/) — who the copy is for: the keystones and inciting events this skill mines for headlines come from the ideal-customer work.
