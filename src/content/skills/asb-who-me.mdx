## What this is about

The right strategy — for a product, a company, a career — is the one
whose success hinges on things you already do exceptionally well and
*want* to keep doing for years. But "know thyself" has been the
hardest assignment for two and a half millennia: you're biased, you
carry baggage, and you've spent years rehearsing who you think you
should be. Meanwhile the most powerful facts about you are literally
invisible to you — like the extrovert who drifts effortlessly through
a party and can't explain "how" because for them it isn't a technique.

This skill runs the way out of the jar. It walks you through the
book's self-discovery prompts (*even when I was a kid, I would ___;
whenever I ___, I get lost in the work and feel energized when it
ends*), then the anti-questions (*when I'm faced with ___, I feel it
in the pit of my stomach*), and it assigns the outside-in questions as
homework to people who know you — the perfect-scenario question, the
personal-hell question, and the one that finds your edge: *what are my
strengths I can't see because they come so naturally?*

It's an interrogation, not a survey. Every self-flattering label gets
pressed for the concrete episode behind it. Sanitized motives get one
mandatory pass of "and what's the less presentable version?" — money,
fame, and proving a point are honorable answers here. And every
finding lands in the file the way the book insists: not as a strength
or a weakness but as a *fact of your personality* — "competitive" is a
gift on a sales call and a curse in a team meeting — with the contexts
where it helps and where it quietly ruins things.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-who-me I'm deciding what company to start next, and I keep
picking ideas that impress other founders instead of ideas I'd
actually love running for a decade. Help me figure out who I am first.
```

The skill interviews you in short rounds — prompts, episodes, presses
— and builds `WHO-ME.md` as you go: your drives, your dreads, the
tensions between them, and homework for the friends who can see what
you can't.

One practical note: say where you're working. Name a directory (or
point at your existing files) when you invoke, and the skill keeps the
method's files together there — if you don't, it asks before creating
anything.

## From the source

Two sources form the foundation of this skill:

- As in Chapter 11 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *To Thine Own Self Be True* (sections "Finding Yourself" and "Outside-In") — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the drive prompts, the anti-questions, the three outside-in questions, and the rule that attributes are facts whose strength-or-weakness expression depends on context.
- [**Pivot Points**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/pivot-points/) — the published system: why "strengths and weaknesses" is the wrong frame, the pivot-foot metaphor for building around your fixed facts, and when (rarely) to invest in changing one.

Supporting articles each supply a piece of the mechanism:

- [**Finding Fulfillment**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/fulfillment/) — the Joy/Skill/Need circles and their traps; the source of this skill's energized-or-wrecked check, which catches burnout hiding behind good results.
- [**Being who you are, while becoming better**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/be-yourself/) — the counterweight: embracing your facts doesn't mean you stop striving, and the "motor" itself is a characteristic worth recording.
