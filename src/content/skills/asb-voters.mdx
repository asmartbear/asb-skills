## What this is about

In high-school debate, the last speaker gets three minutes against the
opponent's seven. You can't answer everything — so the winning tactic,
"the Voter," reframes the whole contest: it all comes down to one or
two key points, chosen where you're strongest. Win those, win the
debate, despite losing everything else. Business works the same way:
Steve Jobs's obsession with industrial design made the iPhone dominant
*despite* iTunes being one of the worst pieces of software ever
shipped. The voter didn't fix the weakness — it overpowered it.

Most founders can't name their voter. They'll offer five strengths
("quality, speed, our people…") that every competitor also claims,
or a genuine strength that is nonetheless common — plenty of people
know your platform; plenty of founders like charging premium prices.
Those *shape* a strategy, but they don't make you special. A voter is
different in kind: an idiosyncratic extreme earned through years of
obsession, rare enough that peers would name *you* for it, proven
decisive by the real trade-offs customers accept for it, and powerful
enough that it forces decisions in product, pricing, and market. At
the highest intensity, your ideal customer reads your weaknesses as
*proof* of it — on a handmade marketplace, the imperfection is the
point.

This skill runs your strengths, experiences, and obsessions through
that gauntlet, one candidate at a time, and refuses to stop at a
comfortable list. It caps the answer at two. It presses every soft
claim — hard — until the answer is specific and solid. And when
nothing survives, it says so plainly: nothing is special-and-extreme
yet, and here's the twofold response — invest your deepest genuine
obsession into extremity, or change games to one you're already built
to win.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-voters Here's my WHO-ME.md and our STRENGTHS-WEAKNESSES.md. I
think our voter is "craftsmanship" but honestly every agency says
that. What actually makes us the ones to bet on?
```

The skill harvests every candidate from your files and stories, runs
each through the five-test gauntlet, and you walk away with
`VOTERS.md` — at most two voters with their evidence and consequences,
plus the strategy audit that starts from them.

One practical note: say where you're working. Name a directory (or
point at your existing files) when you invoke, and the skill keeps the
method's files together there — if you don't, it asks before creating
anything.

## From the source

Two sources form the foundation of this skill:

- As in Chapter 11 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *To Thine Own Self Be True* (section "Play Your Game") — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the Voter tactic itself, the Jobs and Etsy cases, why one or two is the cap, and why weaknesses read as strengths in service of a voter.
- [**Worse, but unique**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/worse-but-unique/) — the King's Gambit argument: a "refuted" strategy wins at your level precisely because nobody prepares for it, and extremity — not mere difference — is what maximizes the few things that make you special.

Supporting articles each supply a piece of the mechanism:

- [**The three kinds of leverage that anchor effective strategies**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/leverage/) — why the story of every winner is one or two things (never the 47 details), and the Teller line this skill uses as its extremity bar: magic is spending more time on something than anyone would reasonably expect.
- [**Pivot Points**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/pivot-points/) — the raw material: the non-judgmental facts of who you are, from which voters are distilled and around which everything else pivots.
