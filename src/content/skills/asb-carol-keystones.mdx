## What this is about

It takes a lot to make someone act — buy new software, rework their
workflows, stake their reputation on you. Sort-of liking a few features
doesn't do it. What does is a **keystone**: something specific about a
customer's circumstances that makes one of your strengths absolutely
critical to them — so critical they think "I've got to make this work
for us." A fast website is a strength; *an e-commerce store where every
tenth of a second of load time measurably moves revenue* is a keystone,
and it names exactly who must buy.

This skill walks your classified strengths one at a time and asks the
chapter's generative question of each: what characteristics, behaviors,
or circumstances *require* an extreme version of this strength? Then
the hard gate: if you can't name a significant real-world market
segment that typifies the keystone, it isn't one — the strength is
table stakes, true but not compelling, and it's cut. What survives is
the map of gravitational pulls that draw customers into your orbit,
each keystone naming its market. And if only one keystone survives —
or none — that's not a failed exercise; it's the most valuable
strategic lesson on the table: your product isn't compelling yet, and
now you know it before the market tells you expensively.

## Example invocation

You can invoke the skill like this:

```text
/asb-carol-keystones Here's our STRENGTHS-WEAKNESSES.md — five
strengths, five weaknesses. Who actually needs what we're best at?
```

The skill takes each strength in turn, presses "who *requires* an extreme version of this?" until the answer is specific, gates every candidate on naming a real market segment, and records the survivors in `KEYSTONES.md` — the file the deal-breakers step hones next.

- [**Selling to Carol: Why targeting an ICP brings 10x more customers than you expected**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/icp-ideal-customer-persona/) — why keystones must concentrate in the best customers and be weak in the worst: a trait common to everyone predicts nothing.

Supporting sources each supply a piece of the mechanism:

- As in Chapter 2 of *Hidden Multipliers*: *Carol Loves You for You* — [hiddenmultipliers.com](https://hiddenmultipliers.com) — the classified strengths chart this skill consumes, and the Profit Whale economics (the top ~5% of customers producing 20–40% of profit) that make finding these customers worth the work.
- [**Specificity: A weapon of mass effectiveness**](https://longform.asmartbear.com/specificity/) — the wording bar: a keystone vague enough to describe everyone describes no one.
