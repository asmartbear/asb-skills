## What this is about

A tiny fraction of your customers generate a wildly outsized share of your
profit — at WP Engine, 5% of customers produced 40% of it. The book calls
the composite of those perfect customers **Carol**, and makes a
counterintuitive claim with two chapters of proof behind it: focusing all
your marketing, positioning, and product development on Carol *alone*
doesn't shrink your market — it wins the ten-times-larger ring of buyers
who share her trade-offs, and the hundred-times-larger ring who weigh them
and pick you anyway. Subaru targeted lesbians in 1994 and became the
fastest-growing car company of the following decade — mostly selling to
people who weren't gay, because clear trade-offs confidently stated win
everyone who leans your way.

But Carol can't be conjured from wishes. She is derived, step by step,
from an honest accounting of who you actually are: your real strengths and
real weaknesses, refined into the keystones that compel a purchase, the
deal-breakers that forbid one, and the inciting events that turn a
someday-buyer into a today-buyer. This process — from *Carol Loves You for
You* and *Speaking Carol Into Existence*, Chapters 2 and 3 of
*[Hidden Multipliers](https://hiddenmultipliers.com)* — runs that
derivation as six skills, each producing a file the next one consumes, so
the work compounds instead of evaporating after a workshop.

## How to run it

1. **Get the raw facts on the record.**
   [Observations](/skills/asb-carol-observations/) walks you (or processes
   your team's write-storm) through twelve unsparing question categories —
   what customers praise, what they leave over, what you claim but aren't,
   what you envy — producing `OBSERVATIONS.md`: vivid, specific raw
   material, deliberately unjudged.
2. **Distill and classify.**
   [Strengths & Weaknesses](/skills/asb-carol-strengths/) converts
   observations into the deep-truth attributes underneath, kills the
   generic ones with the Opposite Test, and classifies each as strength,
   weakness, or deliberately both — `STRENGTHS-WEAKNESSES.md`.
3. **Refine strengths into keystones.**
   [Keystones](/skills/asb-carol-keystones/) asks of each strength: whose
   circumstances *require* an extreme version of this? A strength that
   names no real market segment is table stakes, not a keystone —
   `KEYSTONES.md`.
4. **Refine weaknesses into deal-breakers.**
   [Deal-Breakers](/skills/asb-carol-dealbreakers/) maps who is
   disqualified no matter how much they like you — your anti-market — and
   uses those exclusions to sharpen each keystone market's description —
   `DEALBREAKERS.md`, plus honed `KEYSTONES.md`.
5. **Find the inciting events.**
   [Inciting Events](/skills/asb-carol-inciting-events/) identifies what
   moves a perfect-fit customer from "priority seven" to "buying today" —
   harvested from your customer interviews when you have them, worked
   backward from the keystones when you don't — `INCITING-EVENTS.md`.
6. **Define Carol.**
   [Define Carol](/skills/asb-carol-define/) synthesizes everything into
   `CAROL.md`: the crisp, behavioral definition of your ideal customer —
   attributes first, because that's the point — with reference sections on
   keystones, deal-breakers, and inciting events for the positioning,
   marketing, and product work that starts from it.

The output feeds everything downstream: positioning speaks Carol's
language, marketing buys her attention, sales qualifies against her
markers, and product builds what thrills her. And if you then want to
*test* what you've written down, the
[Customer Interview Method](/processes/customer-interview-method/) is the
companion process — its first goal question is "What does Carol look
like?", and its findings report feeds directly back into these files.
